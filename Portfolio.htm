I am a CompTIA-certified Information Technology professional with a passion for creativity and the arts. I have an extensive professional background in non-profit technology, public health, data management, research, engineering for developing communities, and human rights advocacy.

Through my work as an administrator, engineer, health care volunteer and human rights advocate, I have helped to offer sustainable, community-driven technical solutions to non-profit organizations and the vulnerable populations they serve in the Americas and South Asia. Over the last decade and a half, I have had the great pleasure of working with organizations such as Engineers Without Borders USA and Boulder County AIDS Project. I believe in hard work and enjoying success as a team.

I am a native English speaker with beginning proficiency in Spanish and American Sign Language (ASL). I am also learning French and how to code using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, and Ruby on Rails.

Volunteering in my community is very important to me - everything from working as a cook in soup kitchens to talking with other women who are interested in technology careers. As I live, learn and pursue my passions, I hope to help others do the same. I also love to explore new recipes in the kitchen and grow my own food. I play the cello and bass, collect comic books, and enjoy playing video and board games. I prefer to read books made of paper and love characters with a great backstory. My favorite things to do are: travel, draw, take photos of everything using my vintage Polaroid camera, and hold my face up towards the sun.

This website is growing, so please come back to see more!
